Using my strengths

to carve out a new career

You probably think translation couldn’t be more different from programming. This is generally true. But there are a few shared skills both these professions rely on. I’ve used them to my advantage along my journey into the world of programming.

Translating my skills into code

Here’s a brief summary of the transferable skills that have helped me transition from translation to programming.
Language skills Excellent knowledge of English has allowed me to tap into the collective expertise of thousands of programming experts and made it easier for me to learn the syntax of Java and SQL
Communication skills The ability to communicate effectively (also in English) has been crucial for staying on the same page with other team members and both providing and receiving feedback
Teamwork ability Being part of a team has been a key part of my career as a translator, as I collaborated with my colleagues to deliver superior quality products and ensure client satisfaction
Problem solving Translation is really about solving one linguistic problem after another. I've learned to dissect problems into manageable chunks, and to approach them with creativity and persistence.
Time management With tight deadlines, you need to be extremely well-organised to stay on top of things. Throughout the years I have developed routines and strong discipline to help me beat the clock
Work ethic Commitment to continued self-development and strong belief in the value of hard work have allowed me to face challenges confidently and with gritty determination
